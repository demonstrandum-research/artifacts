# Refutation of Conjecture A.16 of arXiv:2412.16177 (LLM-generated solubilizer conjecture)

**Slug:** `solubilizer-a16` · **Status:** REFUTED (Gate-4 verified 2026-06-11; Gate-5 write-up 2026-06-11)

---

## Abstract (~120 words)

Conjecture A.16 of "Mining Math Conjectures from LLMs: A Pruning Approach" (Chuharski,
Rojas Collins, Meringolo; arXiv:2412.16177) asserts that for every non-solvable group G and
every x in G with Sol_G(x) a proper subgroup, the intersection of Sol_G(x) with its
normalizer in G is metabelian. We refute it. In G = A5 × S4 (order 1440) with
x = ((0 1 2 3 4), 1), brute force over all 1440 elements gives Sol_G(x) = D10 × S4, a
proper self-normalizing subgroup; the intersection is therefore D10 × S4 itself, with
derived series of orders 240, 60, 4, 1 — derived length 3, not metabelian. A 32-check
exact-arithmetic Python checker, mutation-tested and independently reproduced by a second
model family, verifies everything in under 20 seconds. The construction generalizes:
(simple) × (solvable of derived length ≥ 3).

## Result

**Statement refuted (verbatim).** Conjecture A.16, Appendix A.5.6 "Additional Conjectures",
arXiv:2412.16177 v1 (PDF page 13):

> "Let G be a non-solvable group. For any element x in G, if SolG(x) is a proper subgroup
> of G, then the intersection of SolG(x) with its normalizer in G is always metabelian."

with the paper's own definition (Sec. 3.1): Sol_G(x) := {y ∈ G | ⟨x, y⟩ is soluble}.
"Metabelian" is taken in its standard sense (H'' = 1, derived length ≤ 2); the paper defines
no alternative. The conjecture states "non-solvable group" with no finiteness or simplicity
restriction, and our finite G qualifies.

**Provenance.** Jake Chuharski, Elias Rojas Collins, Mark Meringolo, *Mining Math
Conjectures from LLMs: A Pruning Approach*, arXiv:2412.16177 v1 (9 Dec 2024).
PDF https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.16177 and HTML https://arxiv.org/html/2412.16177v1, both
accessed 2026-06-11; snapshots frozen in this directory (`paper.pdf`, `paper.html`,
`paper_pdf.txt`, `paper.txt`, extraction scripts `extract_pdf.py` / `extract_paper.py`).
The PDF's A.x numbering is authoritative (the HTML uses unnumbered conjecture environments
under A1.SS5.SSS6; same verbatim text). A.16 sits in the paper's list of Claude-generated
conjectures "that had code that was unable to be run" — i.e. it was **never
machine-validated by the authors**.

**Result.** The conjecture is FALSE. There is a finite non-solvable group G and x ∈ G with
Sol_G(x) proper such that Sol_G(x) ∩ N_G(Sol_G(x)) has derived length 3.

## The counterexample (inline, complete)

All permutations act on {0,…,8}: A5 on points 0–4, S4 on points 5–8.

- **Group.** G = A5 × S4, |G| = 1440, generated by
  (0 1 2 3 4), (0 1 2), (5 6), (5 6 7 8)
  (one-line images in `certificate.json`; the checker verifies G equals
  {even perms of 0..4} × {all perms of 5..8} element-by-element).
  G is non-solvable: its derived series stalls at order 60 ([1440, 720, 240, 60]).
- **Element.** x = (0 1 2 3 4) with identity S4-part.
- **Solubilizer (brute force over all 1440 y).** Sol_G(x) = D10 × S4, order 240, where
  D10 = ⟨(0 1 2 3 4), (1 4)(2 3)⟩ ≤ A5. Generators:
  (0 1 2 3 4), (1 4)(2 3), (5 6), (5 6 7 8). It is product-closed (a genuine subgroup —
  not automatic for solubilizers) and **proper** (240 < 1440), so A.16's hypothesis holds.
- **Normalizer.** N_G(Sol_G(x)) = Sol_G(x) (self-normalizing; D10 is maximal in A5, hence
  self-normalizing there). So I := Sol_G(x) ∩ N_G(Sol_G(x)) = D10 × S4.
- **The kill.** Derived series of I: D10 × S4 → C5 × A4 → 1 × V4 → 1, orders
  [240, 60, 4, 1]. The second derived subgroup is exactly
  I'' = 1 × V4 = {e, (5 6)(7 8), (5 7)(6 8), (5 8)(6 7)} ≠ 1.
  Hence I has derived length 3 and is **not metabelian**. Conjecture A.16 is refuted.

**Why it works (verified, not assumed).** For y = (u, v), ⟨x, y⟩ embeds in ⟨a, u⟩ × ⟨v⟩ and
surjects onto ⟨a, u⟩, so ⟨x, y⟩ is soluble iff ⟨a, u⟩ is soluble in A5 (the S4-part is
always soluble); and Sol_A5((0 1 2 3 4)) = D10. This subdirect lemma was not taken on
faith: the checker compares it pointwise against the full G-level brute force for all
1440 elements (check 22). The template generalizes: (any simple group with a proper
solubilizer) × (any solvable group of derived length ≥ 3).

## Extension findings

1. **Subdirect lemma verified computationally, not just structurally:** for all 1440
   y = (u, v) ∈ G, ⟨x, y⟩ is soluble in G iff ⟨a, u⟩ is soluble in A5 (check 22, pointwise
   against an independent A5-level brute force). The G-level brute force met 51 distinct
   subgroups ⟨x, y⟩; solvability decided by the generic derived-series test, cached by
   frozenset.
2. **Robust to the grammatical ambiguity "its normalizer":** under the alternative reading
   N_G(⟨x⟩) instead of N_G(Sol_G(x)), the normalizer is again D10 × S4 = Sol (⟨x⟩ has
   order 5; checks 30–32), so the intersection — and the kill — are identical under either
   reading.
3. **The general template:** (simple group with proper solubilizer) × (solvable of derived
   length ≥ 3). The positive control A5 × S3 (derived length 2 factor) satisfies A.16,
   confirming the derived-length-3 factor is what the conjecture's validation sweep — which
   iterated mainly simple groups, where direct products never appear — could never see.
4. **Freshness:** Codex's live web search at Gate 4 (2026-06-11) found no existing
   refutation or proof of A.16; the nearest literature item (Mousavi's
   normalizer-solubilizer conjecture) is a different divisibility statement.

## What this does not show

- It does **not** refute any restriction of A.16 to simple (or almost-simple) groups; in
  A5 itself, Sol_A5(x) = D10 is metabelian. Only the stated general claim ("Let G be a
  non-solvable group") is refuted. Only a reading silently restricting to simple groups
  would dodge the kill, and that is not the written statement.
- It does **not** bear on Mousavi's normalizer-solubilizer conjecture (arXiv:2501.11486),
  which remains open, nor on any other conjecture in arXiv:2412.16177 (A.1 and A.13 are
  refuted separately in sibling directories; the rest are untouched).
- It does **not** claim minimality of the counterexample (|G| = 1440); smaller non-solvable
  examples may exist.
- It is a statement about the conjecture, not about the source paper's main contribution
  (the LLM conjecture-generation pipeline); the authors explicitly flagged A.16 as
  never machine-validated.
- "Metabelian" is interpreted in the standard sense H'' = 1. The paper defines no other
  sense; no standard alternative would rescue the statement here in any case, since the
  derived length is exactly 3 under the only definition in use.
